Modern Era

The Modern Era encompasses the period from the Industrial Revolution to the present day, marked by unprecedented technological advancement, social transformation, and global interconnectedness. This era has fundamentally reshaped human civilization and continues to evolve rapidly.

Defining Characteristics: The Modern Era is characterized by industrialization, urbanization, technological innovation, democratic movements, and the development of a global economy.
Industrial Revolution (1760-1840)

The Industrial Revolution transformed society from agricultural to manufacturing-based economies. Steam power, mechanization, and factory systems revolutionized production and led to rapid urbanization.

Key Innovation: The steam engine, invented by James Watt, powered factories, trains, and ships, enabling mass production and transportation.
20th Century Transformations

The 20th century brought both tremendous progress and unprecedented challenges. Two world wars reshaped global politics, while innovations in medicine, communication, and transportation connected the world like never before.

1903: First airplane flight
1914-1918: World War I
1929: Stock Market Crash
1939-1945: World War II
1969: Moon landing
1989: Fall of Berlin Wall
